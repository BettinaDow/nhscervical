Dear Joe Doe,

We have recently been asked to remove you from the NHS Cervical Screening Programme by your GP or clinic.

Because of this, we have removed your name from the invitation list. You will not receive any more cervical screening invitations or reminders.

Why you have been removed

You are no longer eligible for cervical screening because you will be over the age of 65 when your next test is due.

You are highly unlikely to develop cervical cancer if you are 65 and over and do not have a recent history of abnormal screening results.

More information

Speak to a GP as soon as possible if you have any of the following symptoms, regardless of your cervical screening history:

  • vaginal bleeding that’s unusual for you – including bleeding during or after sex, between your periods or after the menopause, or heavier periods than usual
  • changes to your vaginal discharge
  • pain during sex
  • pain in your lower back, between your hip bones (pelvis), or in your lower tummy

Find more about cervical screening at: www.nhs.uk/cervical


If you think our information is incorrect or you have any questions, contact a GP or your nearest sexual health clinic.
