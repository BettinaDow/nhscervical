If you have not had a cervical screening test since your 60th birthday, you can choose to attend even without an invitation by speaking to a GP or sexual health clinic.

You are highly unlikely to develop cervical cancer if you are 65 and over and do not have a recent history of abnormal screening results.

More information

Speak to a GP as soon as possible if you have any of the following symptoms, regardless of your cervical screening history:

  • vaginal bleeding that’s unusual for you – including bleeding during or after sex, between your periods or after the menopause, or heavier periods than usual
  • changes to your vaginal discharge
  • pain during sex
  • pain in your lower back, between your hip bones (pelvis), or in your lower tummy
