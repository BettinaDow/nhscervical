Dear Joe Doe,

We have received an instruction to withdraw you from the NHS Cervical Screening Programme. We have removed your name from the invitation list and we confirm that you will receive no further invitations for screening from us.

You can return to the cervical screening programme at any time providing that you remain eligible for cervical screening.

If you would like more information about cervical screening, please visit:

www.nhs.uk/cervical or scan the QR code at the top of this letter with your smartphone or device to go straight to the website. The ‘Helping you decide’ leaflet is available here: www.gov.uk/government/publications/cervical-screening-description-in-brief

An easy read version can be found at: www.gov.uk/government/publications/cervical- screening-easy-read-guide

If you have any unusual symptoms, please tell your GP as soon as possible. These might include:

  • vaginal bleeding that is unusual for you - bleeding during or after sex, between your periods, after the menopause or heavier periods than usual
  • pain during sexual intercourse
  • changes to vaginal discharge
  • pain in your lower back, between your hip bones (pelvis), or in your lower tummy

These symptoms are often caused by other things, but it is important to get them checked. If you are found to have cervical cancer, getting it diagnosed and treated early can mean you are more likely to survive.

If you think our information is incorrect, or you have any questions, please speak with your GP. You may wish to retain a copy of this letter for future reference.
